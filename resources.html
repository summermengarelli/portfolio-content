No matter if the Pomodoro method helps you or not, you may feel overwhelmed, stressed or anxious about all that you have to do. This page exists to help you find some resources on the Pomodoro technique, productivity, mental health and wellbeing.

To learn more about the Pomodoro method, read this article from The Everygirl, or this one from Forbes. Also feel free to experiment with what works best for you â€“ you may find that setting the timer for a longer or shorter period of time is more effective for your productivity and wellbeing.

The University of Michigan provides many resources for wellbeing. Check out the CAPS (Counseling and Psychological Services) website to find the therapist for your school or department. UMSI's embedded psychologist is Ashley Evearitt, with whom you can schedule an appointment through this link. Visit the University of Michigan's student wellbeing website to find other resources and events on campus. Finally, GEO (Graduate Employees' Organization) maintains a webpage regarding mental health co-pays for graduate student workers.
